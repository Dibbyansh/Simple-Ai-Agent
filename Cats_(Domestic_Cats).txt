Domestic cats (Felis catus) are small obligate carnivores, the only domesticated
species in the Felidae family. They possess retractable claws, flexible bodies,
sharp teeth, and exceptional senses. Social yet solitary hunters, they are
crepuscular and communicate via vocalizations, body language, and pheromones.
Females produce litters of 2-5 kittens seasonally. Domestication began ~7500 BCE
in the Near East; today, ~600 million exist globally, with Asia (400 million),
the US (73.8 million), and the UK (10.9 million) having the largest populations.
Feral cats threaten wildlife, and spaying/neutering are key population controls.
Cats also serve as working animals and pedigree show contestants.
